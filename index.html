Discussion:

This is a tool meant for people playing any games in the Pokemon franchise that will help them create teams of 6 Pokemon. It provides means of analyzing individuals, as well as whole teams. It also helps players find Pokemon by highlighting their locations on in-game maps.

Over the course of this project, we may have gone a little overboard in adding features. We were just having so much fun that we wanted to keep going until the very last minute. Matt discovered a newfound love for Data Scraping. We both got a lot of experience working in D3, and other Javascript libraries. We also got some more exposure to cleaning incomplete or dirty datasets.

For anyone curious about data scraping, we both recommend looking at the Pokemon Dataset, as it provides a good mix of intuitive data, but it isn't so straightforward as to be trivial to obtain.

Credits:

The Pokemon Dataset on Kaggle was a great starting dataset. While it may not be nearly as "complete" as its name implies, it was a great starting point.

Serebii.net was an exellent resource for data scraping. We used this to correct, enhance, and expand our base dataset with evolution, stat, location, and sprite data

Bulbapedia is a comprehensive source on all things Pokemon. We got our maps from here, in addition to a lot of contextual in game knowledge.

Tabulator was an instrumental tool in constructing our table data.
